BreastCancerAI

A deep learning platform for histopathology image analysis. Built on EfficientNetB4 transfer learning to assist in distinguishing benign from malignant breast tissue samples.

Research Prototype — Not for Clinical Use
How It Works
1
Image Upload
Upload a histopathology slide image in PNG, JPG, or TIFF format. Whole-slide SVS tiles are also accepted.
2
Preprocessing
The image is resized to 224×224, normalised, and prepared as a batch tensor matching the model's expected input.
3
Deep Feature Extraction
EfficientNetB4 — pre-trained on ImageNet — extracts rich convolutional features from cellular morphology patterns.
4
Binary Classification
A fully-connected sigmoid head outputs a probability score. Above 0.5 is classified as Malignant; below as Benign.
5
Report & Recommendations
A structured pathology summary and clinical recommendations are generated based on the prediction and confidence score.

Known Limitations
Model performance is highly dependent on dataset quality and size. Small or unbalanced training sets will degrade accuracy.
Predictions are based on visual patterns only — no clinical metadata, patient history, or molecular markers are considered.
The model may not generalise well to images from staining protocols or scanner equipment not represented in its training data.
Confidence scores reflect model certainty, not clinical probability. A 95% confidence score does not mean 95% clinical accuracy.
Medical Disclaimer
BreastCancerAI is an academic research prototype developed for educational and experimental purposes only. It is not a certified medical device, has not been validated in clinical settings, and must not be used to inform patient care or clinical decisions without review by a qualified pathologist or oncologist. Always consult licensed medical professionals for diagnosis and treatment.
